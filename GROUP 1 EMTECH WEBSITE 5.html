Our Story

Every Flavor Tells a Story

Kusina De Nueva Castilla Restaurant, located in the heart of Pasig City, is more than just a place to eat—it's a living memory of friendship, flavor, and shared dreams. Known for its unique dishes, friendly service, and consistent quality, the restaurant stands as a testament to the bond of seventeen classmates who turned their love for food into a successful business.

Back in their high school days, Reymond N. Magayanes, Aeron Nathaniel L. Cammagay, Jaymart E. Campuso, Mark Albos R. Conde, Kevin Russel M. Dela Rosa, Jhon Mark F. Evangelista, Jhan Philip C. Garalda, Adrian James A. Malasibas, Jhon Dhael B. Montano, Alwyn P. Tumlos, Sheenna Mea J. Acuin, Francheska Andrea R. Cabaddu, Kylen J. Dumdumaya, Sofia Nicole S. Garino, Crissa M. Montesino, Rhiane Cassandra S. Ronidel, and Erich F. Teriote were inseparable. After long hours of classes, they would head out together to explore food spots around the city—laughing over shared meals, rating dishes like food critics, and dreaming about running their own restaurant someday. Years later, a reunion idea—"What if we build our own restaurant?"—turned into a shared dream and a plan.

When the seventeen friends decided to build Kusina De Nueva Castilla, each brought a strength. Rhiane Cassandra S. Ronidel loved experimenting with flavors; Jhon Mark F. Evangelista learned practical kitchen skills working in small eateries; Francheska Andrea R. Cabaddu brought creative plating and presentation. Reymond N. Magayanes organized and led projects, Erich F. Teriote handled business strategies, and Mark Albos R. Conde kept operations resourceful and efficient. Kylen J. Dumdumaya, Crissa M. Montesino, Kevin Russel M. Dela Rosa, and Aeron Nathaniel L. Cammagay shaped the restaurant’s cozy look and branding. Sheenna Mea J. Acuin, Sofia Nicole S. Garino, Jhon Dhael B. Montano, and Jaymart E. Campuso created the warm hospitality that makes guests feel at home. Jhan Philip C. Garalda, Alwyn P. Tumlos, and Adrian James A. Malasibas kept the finances balanced and planned for growth.

Today, Kusina De Nueva Castilla is celebrated not only for its food but for the story behind it: a story of friendship, teamwork, and shared dreams. From high school food trips to owning a beloved restaurant, these seventeen friends proved that with unity and passion, anything is possible.
